Desalination plant

High Pressure pumps with energy recuperator for desalination

overview product range data sheets related product

The axial-plunger HP pump with integrated energy recovery was created without tubes and pipelines. All four channels are placed on the pump back panel. APPR series axial-plunger pump is made of Duplex and Super Duplex steel. The pump has no rolling bearings. All the friction pairs are made of Super Duplex steel and PEEK polymer. Seawater feeding is performed 50% by plungers moving with the rotor from electric drive along the inclined disc, and 50% by mobile hubs (made of PEEK) installed on the plungers. The hubs are driven by the pressure of high-pressure brine bled from the membrane elements.

The PEEK covered distribution disc with four channels distributes the fluid flows. During module development, the necessity arose to make the membrane body not so conventional. To achieve the normal operation in absence of tubes all three channels (high-pressure water, brine, permeate) were placed on the front panel of the body.

  • APPR-38 3,1 m3/h

    High pressure pump with integrated energy recovery exchanger

    Outlet rated pressure: 55 bar (785 psi)

    Outlet maximum pressure: 70 bar (1000 psi)

    Rotation speed: 1000 - 1700 rpm.

    Flow at speed 1465 rpm: 3.3 m3/h (13 gpm)

    Driving motor power at rated pressure: 4,0 kW

    APPR pumps can be used for salt or brackish water and are designed for applying in reverse osmosis systems. APPR pumps are the most advanced pumps in the world that contain an internal energy recovery device, therefore APPR is both a high-pressure pump and an energy exchanger.

    Thanks to the energy exchanger energy consumption are reduced more than two times if compare with analog high-pressure piston pumps.

    All metal parts are made of Duplex and Super Duplex steel that has extreme resistance to corrosion.  Non-metallic parts are made of PEEK polymer, which is also highly corrosion-resistant and wear-resistant.

    The pressure is regulated by an integrated adjustable valve, screwing of the valve increases the pressure and unscrewing of the valve decreases the pressure.
